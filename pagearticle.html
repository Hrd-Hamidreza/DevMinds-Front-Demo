How to plan to learn programming?

Other

Farhad Kazemzadeh

2023-08-08

1527 views

Planning to learn programming is the most important step in the path of learning this profession. With proper planning, you can get the best results from your time and energy in learning programming. Planning to learn programming is to determine the amount of time, resources, and learning goals. By determining the appropriate goals and path, you can find the best way to learn programming and achieve success in the shortest possible time. In this planning, the topics you plan to learn and the exercises you need to do should be specified, and specific times should be considered for learning, studying, and doing exercises.

In this article, we present the most important methods that you should consider in your planning: 1. Weekly planning: To learn programming, you can make a weekly plan. In this plan, for each week, you can specify the topics you plan to learn and the exercises you need to do. For each exercise, estimate the maximum time required and include it in your plan. 2. Selection of resources: To learn programming, you need to look for the right resources to learn from. You can use books, videos, online tutorials, articles, and other resources. When choosing resources, it is best to use resources that are verified by professionals and experts in the programming industry. 3. Carrying out practical projects: To learn programming, you need to put your skills into practical use. So, after learning the basics of programming, it is better to start practical projects. You can start with small projects on topics like simple calculations, simple games, and data management applications, and gradually move on to more advanced and larger projects. 4. Participate in the programming community: Participating in the programming community can help you improve your skills and meet people in the programming industry. To participate in the programming community, you can join forums, programming groups, social sites, and messaging apps. 5. Determining progress criteria: To better plan your programming learning, you need to set your own progress metrics. You can consider metrics like the number of exercises you have completed, projects you have completed, your level of programming language proficiency, the amount of code you have written, etc. By setting your own progress metrics, you can assess your progress in learning programming and make necessary improvements to your planning. 6. Setting specific times: To better plan your programming learning, you should set aside specific times to learn, study, and practice. These times can be daily, weekly, or monthly and should be included in your weekly schedule. 7. Being flexible: When planning your programming lessons, you need to be flexible and allow for changes in your schedule. If you can't complete all the exercises in one week, you can move them to the next week. Also, if you encounter a complex topic while learning, you can change your schedule and focus on simpler topics. 8. Review and Evaluation: To better plan your programming learning, you should consider reviewing and evaluating your progress. It is best to review your weekly plan every week and evaluate your progress. Also, you should pay attention to your progress metrics and make necessary improvements to your plan.
